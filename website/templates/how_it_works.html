Design decisions

Why we built it this way

Every architectural choice is intentional. Here's the reasoning behind them.

Two inputs, not one

Separate inputs mean there's no ambiguity in what you're comparing. No parsing "vs" from a single string. The pair is normalised alphabetically so the cache always hits regardless of order.

AI synthesis, not just search

Raw search results are noisy and contradictory. Gemini reads all of it and extracts a structured verdict — with the model explicitly instructed to be opinionated and cite its sources, not hedge.

Shared result cache

The second person to compare Notion vs Obsidian gets the same cached result as the first. No redundant scraping, no repeated API costs — and it gets faster the more popular the pair.

Multiple data sources

Tavily handles broad web coverage. Hacker News provides high-signal technical opinion from practitioners. Using both means the model sees a more complete picture before forming a verdict.

No sponsored content

Wherelse doesn't take money from the products it compares. The AI doesn't know which vendor would prefer to win — and neither does our database. The verdict is based on what people actually say.

Structured JSON output

Asking Gemini for JSON instead of prose means every comparison has consistent fields. You can always expect a TL;DR, verdict, pros/cons, and a use-case recommendation — never a wall of unstructured text.

FAQ

Common questions

How fresh is the data?
New comparisons are freshly scraped at time of request. Cached comparisons show the result from when they were first generated. We're building automatic cache invalidation so popular comparisons re-run periodically to stay current.
Can I compare anything, or just software?
The pipeline works best for software tools, SaaS products, developer platforms, and productivity apps — that's where community discussion is richest. Comparing physical products or services with thin online discussion will produce lower-quality results, and the sources_used field will reflect that.
How long does a new comparison take?
Typically 10–30 seconds for a cold request, depending on how much source data needs processing. Cached results are near-instant. You'll see a loading state while the pipeline runs.
Is the verdict actually unbiased?
It's as unbiased as the sources we pull from. The AI synthesises what real users and communities have said — it doesn't have a horse in the race. We don't take money from vendors, and the model is explicitly instructed to be honest rather than diplomatic.
Do I need an account?
No. Comparisons are free and public. An account lets you save comparisons to your history and revisit them later, but the core comparison feature works without signing up.
